# Evidence Base

The research findings behind the playbook, for when the user wants justification or a strategy document with sources. Numbers are as reported by the cited sources circa 2025–2026; re-verify with fresh search when precision matters, as this field moves fast.

## The funnel shift

- Customers' research phase now happens inside AI assistants that synthesize multiple sources; visitors arrive at websites already prequalified. Practitioners report organic-blog traffic collapsing (one documented agency case: −60%) while sales-ready leads held steady or grew after shifting to AEO (Entrepreneur magazine, May–June 2026, "How to Make AI Recommend You").
- E-commerce sites measured LLM-referred traffic converting at ~5.53% vs ~3.7% for organic search (Amsive) — AI-referred buyers arrive pre-sold.
- Overlap between top-10 Google results and AI-cited sources fell from ~70% to under 20% (Brandlight) — ranking on Google no longer implies AI visibility, and vice versa.

## What gets cited

- **Earned-media bias:** an arXiv GEO study (Sept 2025) found AI search systematically prefers third-party, authoritative sources over brand-owned content. Corroboration on Reddit, Wikipedia, review platforms, or editorial press is close to a prerequisite for confident citation.
- **Reddit** was the most-cited domain by LLMs in 2025–2026 — above Wikipedia.
- **Freshness:** ~83% of AI citations for commercial/evaluation queries came from pages updated within 12 months; 60%+ within 6 months (AirOps).
- **Fact density:** adding statistics, citations, and quotations lifted visibility of lower-ranked sites by up to ~40% in AI responses (GEO research).
- **Entity clarity:** AI cross-checks Knowledge Graph, Wikidata, schema, and social profiles for agreement on who a brand is before trusting it.

## What works on-page

- Lead with the answer: AI engines reward pages whose opening directly answers the question; thin 50-word descriptions are described by practitioners as "a dead end."
- Hyperspecific pages matching query shape ("[service] for [industry] in [city]") get recommended because AI excels at precise query-to-page matching; the pages need to be crawlable, not to rank (Vital Design methodology, Entrepreneur May–June 2026).
- Structured data (Product, FAQPage, Organization, HowTo) is the mechanism by which AI knows price, availability, and identity.
- Curated category/"best of" pages are the largest e-commerce AEO opportunity because "best X for Y" is the dominant commercial AI query shape; honest inclusion of competitors increases trust and citation likelihood.

## Measurement reality

- Attribution is structurally leaky: research in the AI, conversion later and direct. Compensate with post-purchase surveys, AI-domain referral segmentation, monthly fixed-query re-tests against a dated baseline, and lead-quality (not traffic) as the success metric.
- Monitoring tools exist at multiple tiers (entry ~$29/mo; mid ~$95/mo with optimization signals; enterprise ~$99+/mo) — e.g., Otterly.ai, AthenaHQ, Profound.

## Younger-demographic overlay (when the audience skews Gen Z / Gen Alpha)

- Discovery starts in algorithmic feeds, not search: ~43% of Gen Z start product searches on TikTok; ~44% have used TikTok Shop; review videos drive purchases for ~53%.
- Gen Alpha discovery: YouTube ~56%, TikTok ~22%; ~48% of 11–14s discover products via influencers; purchases remain parent-mediated — the parent's verification step (searching or asking AI "is [brand] good?") is where AEO intersects the youth journey.
- Implication: social creates the demand spike; AEO/reviews convert the validation traffic it generates. They are complements, not alternatives.
